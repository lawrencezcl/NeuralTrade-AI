## Q&A Preparation

### Expected Questions & Answers

**Q1: How do you ensure the AI doesn't make bad trades?**

"A: Great question. We have multiple safety layers:
1. Risk Engine calculates position sizing based on Kelly Criterion
2. Stop-loss and take-profit automatically set
3. Daily volume limits prevent overexposure
4. Confidence threshold - only trades above 75% execute
5. Emergency pause for manual intervention
6. All trades can be reviewed and reverted"

**Q2: What makes this different from existing trading bots?**

"A: Three key differentiators:
1. True AI - not just rules, but ML models that learn and adapt
2. Natural language interface - no coding required
3. Multi-strategy approach - not limited to one strategy
4. Built specifically for Injective's unique features"

**Q3: How do you handle market crashes?**

"A: Our risk management activates automatically:
1. Dynamic stop-losses tighten
2. Position sizes reduce
3. Cash reserves increase
4. User can trigger emergency pause
5. Historical training includes crash scenarios"

**Q4: What's your business model?**

"A: We envision multiple revenue streams:
1. Performance fees on profits
2. Premium strategies marketplace
3. Enterprise/API access
4. Strategy copy trading (social trading)
5. White-label for institutions"

**Q5: What are the main risks?**

"A: We're transparent about risks:
1. Smart contract risk - mitigated by audits and bug bounties
2. AI model risk - addressed by ensemble methods and human oversight
3. Market risk - inherent to trading, we manage but can't eliminate
4. Oracle risk - using multiple price feeds for reliability"
